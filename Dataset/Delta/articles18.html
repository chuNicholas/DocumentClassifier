COVID-19: 69 new cases over weekend; Delta variant on its way to becoming more dominant strain

Of the 12,312 variants of concern detected in the province to date, only 7,807 have been sequenced.

Author of the article:

The Delta variant continues to strengthens its foothold in Saskatchewan.

As the provinces enters its second week without restrictions, Saskatchewan reported 69 new cases over the weekend while also confirming additional variant of concern cases.

Of the 70 VOC’s identified by whole genome sequencing, through screening of previously reported COVID-19 cases, 47 of them were found to be of the highly contagious and more transmissible Delta (formerly known as Indian) strain.

Of these, all but three were located in the far north east zone, where an outbreak around Hatchet Lake Denesuline First Nation has been linked to more than 150 cases.

Of the remaining VOCs, 11 were identified as Alpha (formerly known as U.K.) and two as Gamma (previously known as South African).

However, thousands of variants have yet to be sequenced in Saskatchewan as the data for continues to lag over time. Of the 12,312 VOC’s detected in the province to date, only 7,807 VOCs have been sequenced. That leaves 4,505 variant cases yet to be categorized.

Of the 69 new COVID cases over the weekend, 36 were reported Sunday and 33 on Saturday. Active cases has dropped to 282, with most of those in either Saskatoon (which leads all zones with 52 cases), far north west (43) or far north east (41).

New cases reported Sunday are located in the far north west (13), Saskatoon (nine), Regina (four) far north east (two), north west (two), north central (two), south west (one), and south central (one) zones. Two cases have pending residence information

Saturday’s cases were reported for the far north west (one), far north east (one), north west (four), north central (10), Saskatoon (12), central east (one), Regina (one), south west (two) and south east (one) zones.

As of Sunday, a total of 53 individuals are hospitalized, including 44 inpatient hospitalizations and nine ICU hospitalizations. Saskatoon, with 52 active cases, has 21 of those hospitalized, five in intensive care. Regina has 18 of its 37 cases — nearly half — hospitalized with three of them in ICU.

No new deaths reported as 575 Saskatchewan residents with COVID-19 have died thus far, with a case fatality rate of 1.2 per cent.

A total of 6,406 vaccine doses were given Saturday with most of those (5,473) being second doses. On Friday, 9,426 shots were administered with 1,569 being used as first doses, according to data posted on the provincial government’s online COVID-19 dashboard.

Daily vaccination totals continue to hit well below the highs seen in June and early July as experts warn of pandemic complacency through the summer months.
