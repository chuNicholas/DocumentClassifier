Swoop launches service between Kelowna and Hamilton

Swooping into Kelowna

Cindy White - Jun 20, 2021 / 5:37 pm | Story: 337586

WestJet's discount carrier Swoop has landed a new offering in Kelowna.

The first flight from Hamilton to YLW arrived Sunday, shortly before 10:30 a.m.

"It's four flights a week," said Sam Samaddar, Kelowna International Airport director.

"Obviously we are very, very happy to have this service out of Hamilton, a community that we haven't served for some time."

Among those arriving was Joanne, who came to Kelowna from St. Catharines to visit someone she hasn't seen in more than a year.

"This is wonderful, oh yeah. We're 15 minutes down the road from my friend's [house]," she said.

Samaddar said there is "pent-up demand" for the flights.

"People want to visit family and friends, but it's also an opportunity to explore your country, Canada, and when you look at Hamilton, there's a lot of similarities between that region and the Okanagan," he said.

Before the pandemic, there were nine Canadian destinations from Kelowna International Airport. Now that things are ramping back up for domestic travel, Samaddar said there are 16 destinations.

International flights haven't resumed, but Samaddar said he's hopeful visitors will start coming back to Kelowna for the sun this summer and the great skiing in the winter.
