Celtic’s transfer swoop for Sacho Boey ‘collapses’ as Rennes starlet walks away amid ‘foreign intermediaries’ issue

  • Ewan Paton
  • 15:04, 19 Jul 2021
  • Updated: 15:04, 19 Jul 2021

SACHO BOEY has walked away from talks with Celtic, according to reports.

Media reports in France say the talented Rennes defender has cited 'foreign intermediaries' as part of his decision to reject the move.

Ange Postecoglou was keen on the ex-Dijon right-back as he kicks up Parkhead rebuild ahead of the Champions League second round qualifier with FC Midtjylland on Tuesday.

L'Equipe say that a deal to bring the 20-year-old to Glasgow's east end was at an advanced stage and talks had been ongoing.

Boey has just one year remaining on his contract and Celtic could add him to their ranks on a cut-price deal.

However, the same report also states that he has rejected the contract offer from Parkhead and walked away from negotiations.

GRIFF RETURN Celtic star Leigh Griffiths back in training and in contention to play versus FC Midtjylland in Champions League

The outlet claims the potential switch move fell apart due to difficulties with "foreign intermediaries" during the discussions.

It is said that a host of French clubs remain interested in the youngster.

Meanwhile, Leigh Griffiths has returned to Celtic training, says Ange Postecoglou.

The Hoops forward is now back in contention for tomorrow's Champions League qualifying clash with FC Midtjylland.
