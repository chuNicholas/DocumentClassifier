Everton triple transfer swoop makes Rafa Benitez plan clear

A number of reports have come to the surface over recent days concerning players that Everton and Rafa Benitez are interested in bringing to the club this summer

Everton's transfer window finally seems to be kicking into life.

And, while on holiday after his exploits with England throughout Euro 2020, Dominic Calvert-Lewin might just be raising his eyebrows at what he's seeing.

Over the last few days it has been revealed that Everton are closing in on their first two deals of the summer, with both Asmir Begovic and Andros Townsend edging towards confirming their respective moves to Goodison Park.

Demarai Gray is another player that interests the Blues, although any potential swoop for the Bayer Leverkusen star isn't as close as the previous duo mentioned.

And, on Monday, the ECHO revealed that Benitez also holds an interest in Burnley's exciting 21-year-old Dwight McNeil - who could cost up to £25m as competition from Aston Villa may exist.

Three out of those four players fit into a very particular mould, one that will surely suit Calvert-Lewin down to the ground.

It's no surprise to see Everton linked with a number of wingers across the course of this transfer window, it's a position that many highlighted as one in desperate need of improvement.

Particularly someone to play on the right flank was required but with the potential for Bernard to leave this summer, as a possible move to the Middle East has been touted, then added quality on both sides could well become the strategy.

Towards the second half of the 2020/21 campaign in particular, crossing ability from those wide areas of the pitch was distinctly lacking for Everton - apart from when Lucas Digne got forward from left back or when James Rodriguez was on the pitch.

Aside from those two, the Blues consistently struggled to centre the ball into the box with much authority, conviction or accuracy.

In turn, that took one of Calvert-Lewin strongest assets away from his arsenal. His leap and heading ability are almost unmatched across the top division.

In fact, the England international still scoring more headed goals than anyone else in the Premier League last term with seven, despite Everton's often inability to find him inside the box on a regular basis.

This is something Benitez has already addressed, speaking highly of his striker just after his first press conference as Blues manager last week.

He said: "It is very easy to see that we have a great centre-forward. He’s very good in the air, he can score goals. But we have to provide him with good crosses, good passes.

"So the objective is to add the players to improve. We can improve in these areas and, from the wide areas, the crosses will come. When we can do that, the team will be better."

Already the manager's aim seems to be coming to fruition.

Andros Townsend is a more experienced head arriving at the club this summer from Crystal Palace, but comes with an interesting stat.

Along with his five assists garnered in the top division last term, the 30-year-old also had the best cross accuracy of anyone in the Premier League - of those who attempted 25 or more balls into the box - with 33%.

Surely that alone would be enough to intrigue Calvert-Lewin.

The addition of someone such as Demarai Gray could also prove to be an astute signing if the Blues were to pursue their interest in the Bundesliga star further.

He might not have experienced the rise some might have envisaged, but the 25-year-old still has a lot of time to progress and could be desperate to prove himself on a big stage once more.

A reported fee of £1.5m could well provide as little risk as possible for Everton too, especially given the current Financial Fair Play situation.

The same can't be said in that case for McNeil, who would command a potential price of £25m for his services, but he would be a player to instantly improve the club's starting 11.

Another player with five Premier League assists last season, the 21-year-old has been highlighted by many in recent years as a potential future star with a lot of room to grow.

He attempted an average of 1.4 crosses per game last season in the top division, very similar to Digne's figure of 1.5.

Those two complementing each other down the left flank could easily be a nightmare for opposition defences to deal with. Both are very capable of placing a cross exactly where they want it in the penalty area.

All of this highlights a potential change in strategy from the Blues that hasn't been seen in a while at Goodison Park.

While Everton have been dangerous down the flanks in recent years, much of that has come from full backs getting forward rather than out-and-out wingers providing crosses into the box.

Instead the club have often preferred to utilise their wide man almost as inside forwards, with stars such as Richarlison picking up the ball from the left and cutting inside to attack through the middle.

While that has worked on occasion, Benitez has clearly highlighted Calvert-Lewin as the club's real danger man. Playing to his strengths is key, and that means quality crosses into the box.

What will be interesting is how this affects the rest of the squad, as this is a clear shift in approach and dynamic across the whole side.

How will the likes of Bernard and Alex Iwobi feel about these incomings, for example? Where will Richarlison's position lie in the future? Which blend of central midfielders will best complement those out wide?

Those are questions for the manager to answer throughout pre-season and into the 2021/22 term, however.

This clear strategy from Everton is a new and potentially exciting direction for the club, if the right players can be brought in for the right prices.

Now we wait to see if these deals can be brought over the line.
